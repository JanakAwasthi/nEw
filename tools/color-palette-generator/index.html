Color Harmony Guide

Monochromatic

Uses different shades, tones, and tints of a single color. Creates a harmonious and calming effect.

Analogous

Uses colors that are next to each other on the color wheel. Creates a sense of harmony and unity.

Complementary

Uses colors opposite each other on the color wheel. Creates high contrast and vibrant look.

Triadic

Uses three colors equally spaced on the color wheel. Offers high contrast while retaining harmony.

Tetradic

Uses four colors arranged into two complementary pairs. Rich in variety with many possibilities.

Split Complementary

Uses a base color and two colors adjacent to its complement. Less tension than complementary.
